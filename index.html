How it's made

The Process

01

Choose the Fabric

Every project starts with fabric I'd actually want to touch. Linen, canvas, cotton, waxed cloth — I source for feel, weight, and color before anything else. The right material makes or breaks the finished piece.

02

Cut, Piece & Sew

No shortcuts. Every piece is cut by hand, pinned, and sewn in my Pennsylvania home studio. Each seam matters. Each stitch placed with intention — usually with a podcast on and a dog at my feet.

03

Ready for Real Life

Finished, inspected, and packed up by hand. I want it to arrive looking like something worth keeping — because it is. Something you'll still be using years from now.

04

And then it’s yours

Every seam sewn with intention. Built to be carried, repaired, and lived in — for years, not seasons. Made the long way around so the piece you receive is one you'll still be using long after.

Meet Molly
"I wanted to make things that outlast the trend they were bought in."

Every bag and quilt is made by hand in my Pennsylvania home studio. What started as a love of fabric — the weight of good canvas, the satisfaction of a perfectly mitered corner — turned into something I couldn't put down.

I make bags people actually use every day, and art quilts that hang on walls and get better the longer you look at them. Not decoration for decoration's sake — pieces with presence. Every seam sewn with intention.

Custom commissions are my favorite part of this work. Tell me what you're looking for and let's figure out what we can make together.

— Molly
